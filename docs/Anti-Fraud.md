## **Anti-Fraud & Suspicious Transaction Monitoring Policy**

**Document Code:** AML-FRD-009  
 **Version:** 4.0  
 **Effective Date:** 01 February 2025  
 **Department:** Financial Crime & Compliance

---

## **1\. Purpose**

To define monitoring, reporting, and escalation procedures for:

* Fraud detection

* Money laundering prevention

* Suspicious transaction reporting

* Internal misconduct

---

## **2\. Suspicious Activity Indicators**

### **2.1 Transaction-Based Indicators**

* Cash deposits \> 1,000,000 DZD without clear justification

* Structuring (multiple small deposits under reporting threshold)

* Sudden foreign transfers to high-risk jurisdictions

* Rapid in/out transactions within 24h

---

### **2.2 Behavioral Indicators**

* Customer avoids answering source-of-funds questions

* Frequent changes in account signatories

* Refusal to provide beneficial ownership info

---

## **3\. Automated Monitoring Rules**

Rule A1:  
 If cash deposits \> 3× monthly average → flag Level 1 alert.

Rule B2:  
 If international transfer \> 5M DZD AND destination high-risk → Level 2 alert.

Rule C3:  
 If account dormant \> 12 months AND sudden inflow \> 2M DZD → escalate.

---

## **4\. Alert Escalation Levels**

Level 1 → Branch Review  
 Level 2 → Compliance Review  
 Level 3 → Financial Crime Unit

Level 3 requires Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) submission within 48 hours.

---

## **5\. STR Submission Procedure**

1. Gather transaction logs

2. Document rationale

3. Attach supporting documents

4. Submit through regulator portal

5. Freeze account if required

---

## **6\. Internal Fraud**

Employee misconduct triggers:

* Immediate system suspension

* Internal audit investigation

* HR disciplinary action

* Possible criminal complaint
